# Nebula Infinity V1 Brand Guidelines

**Version:** V1 positioning reset  
**Applies to:** Public-facing website, design handoff, page copy direction, and supporting visual assets

---

## 0. Brand core

Nebula Infinity should read as a company that helps organizations move from interest in AI to **real operational capability**.

### Approved positioning anchors
These are the strongest brand lines in V1 and should remain intact unless explicitly replaced by a later approved messaging pass:

- **AI社会実装のパートナー**
- **「AI」を、現場の「即戦力」へ**
- **「属人」のノウハウを、組織の「資産」に**
- **AI駆動開発で、超高速実装**

### What the brand should feel like
- credible
- composed
- implementation-minded
- technical but readable
- premium without luxury theatrics
- business-progressive, not speculative

### What the brand should not feel like
- loud
- futuristic-for-its-own-sake
- startup-hype-only
- crypto-trader-coded
- vague about outcomes

## 1. Brand tone

### 1.1 Tone pillars

#### Pillar A — Practical authority
Nebula Infinity should sound like a partner that has shipped real things.

**Express visually through:**
- structured sections
- clear headings and labels
- proof cards, process steps, and capability blocks
- restrained premium styling instead of dramatic effects

**Express in copy through:**
- verbs like `設計`, `実装`, `統合`, `運用`, `定着`, `改善`
- concrete capability language
- short confident claims backed by examples

**Avoid:**
- “revolutionize everything” style copy
- unexplained buzzwords
- oversized promises without proof

#### Pillar B — Structured intelligence
The company should look organized enough to handle complexity.

**Express visually through:**
- bento layouts
- consistent alignment
- clear icon systems
- calm spacing and hierarchy

**Express in copy through:**
- grouping information into systems, phases, categories, and outcomes
- using plain business language before technical detail

**Avoid:**
- chaotic collage layouts
- dense jargon walls
- too many competing visual focal points

#### Pillar C — Asset-minded transformation
The brand's AI workflow story is not just automation. It is about converting tacit, person-dependent know-how into repeatable organizational assets.

**Express visually through:**
- flow diagrams
- before/after structures
- input → system → outcome framing
- cards that show capture, standardization, reuse, and scaling

**Express in copy through:**
- phrases like `属人化の解消`, `ナレッジの資産化`, `再現性`, `運用可能`, `引き継げる仕組み`

**Avoid:**
- framing AI workflow as only coding automation
- presenting current workflow examples as the full service boundary

#### Pillar D — AI-Driven Development
AI-driven delivery should feel fast **and** reliable — speed paired with quality, never reckless.

**Express visually through:**
- security, system, governance, traceability, ledger, identity, and infrastructure metaphors
- the same calm light design system used for AI pages

**Express in copy through:**
- business utility
- traceability
- customer experience improvement
- product/system capability

**Avoid:**
- coins, moonshots, candles, token-price language
- “next 100x” tone
- crypto-native slang as primary website language

## 2. Tone by content type

| Content type | Recommended tone | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | Clear, high-trust, outcome-led | Short, memorable, not metaphor-heavy |
| Hero subtitle | Strategic + practical | Explain who it's for and what changes |
| Service section intro | Consultative | Clarify use cases and business fit |
| Case/proof section | Evidence-led | Use delivered capability, scale, and fit |
| CTA | Calm confidence | Invite discussion, don't pressure |
| Form microcopy | Reassuring and direct | Make next step feel clear and low-friction |
| AI-Driven Development pages | Confident, precise | Stress speed paired with quality and reliability |

### CTA language examples

**Preferred**
- お問い合わせ
- ご相談ください
- 実績を見る
- まずは要件を整理する
- Discuss your use case
- View representative cases

**Avoid**
- 今すぐ革命を始める
- 爆速で未来を手に入れる
- Ape in now
- Launch the next moonshot

## 3. Visual expression of the brand

### 3.1 Primary art direction
**Light Premium Bento** is the correct V1 expression.

That means:
- light page background
- white surfaces
- navy structure
- gold accent
- modular cards
- subtle gradients
- system-like iconography
- clean separation between sections

### 3.2 Secondary visual cues
Allowed supporting cues:
- workflow paths
- nodes and connections
- blueprint or orchestration diagrams
- KPI numerals
- premium badges used sparingly
- trust panels and process ladders

### 3.3 Avoided visual cues
Do not center the brand around:
- glossy 3D AI heads
- generic robot mascots
- neon purple/pink cyberpunk gradients
- outer-space clichés used literally across all pages
- stock photos of people pointing at laptops
- speculative crypto charts or coin piles

## 4. Icon style

### 4.1 Icon direction
Use a **rounded outline icon system** that feels technical, precise, and lightweight.

### 4.2 Icon specification

| Property | Rule |
|---|---|
| Viewbox | `24 x 24` |
| Stroke width | `1.7px-1.9px` |
| Stroke style | Rounded linecap + rounded linejoin |
| Fill | Prefer none; use fill only for tiny emphasis dots or small closed accents |
| Corner feel | Soft geometric, not playful blob shapes |
| Default sizes | 20px, 22px, 24px, 32px |
| Wrapper sizes | 40px, 44px, 48px, 64px |

### 4.3 Recommended icon set reference
- **Primary recommendation:** Lucide-style geometry as the reference standard.
- **Implementation preference:** inline SVG in the codebase for consistency, translation safety, and styling control.

### 4.4 Icon usage rules
- Wrap icons in a soft gold-tinted square or circle for important cards.
- Keep icons monochrome and inherit `currentColor`.
- Use icons to explain **systems, flows, products, trust, and outcomes**.
- Decorative icons should be `aria-hidden="true"`.

### 4.5 Icon don'ts
- no emojis
- no photo-based icons
- no heavy filled icon set as the primary style
- no different icon families mixed on the same page

## 5. Illustration direction

### 5.1 Preferred illustration style
Use **abstract system illustrations** rather than character illustrations.

Recommended motifs:
- workflow diagrams
- connected modules
- data pipelines
- layered cards and dashboards
- abstract ledgers / networks / orchestration maps
- directional arrows showing capture → structure → reuse

### 5.2 Rendering style
- flat or light pseudo-isometric
- thin strokes or subtle fills
- restrained gold accents
- mostly neutral background massing
- minimal noise and no high-saturation glow

### 5.3 Placement guidance

| Placement | Rule |
|---|---|
| Hero side visual | One strong support illustration max |
| Section dividers | Small abstract cues only |
| Empty states | Light, calm, helpful |
| Case/proof cards | Product/UI screenshots preferred over abstract art when available |
| Backgrounds | Keep decorative art below content hierarchy |

### 5.4 If screenshots are available
Prefer:
- real product UI
- simplified crop of real systems
- crisp neutral backgrounds
- light framing with border/shadow

Avoid:
- fake dashboards created only for visual noise
- cluttered collage screenshots
- screenshots with unreadable dense text blocks

### 5.5 Illustration don'ts
- no anime or mascot characters
- no astronaut/galaxy imagery as the core language
- no glossy 3D blobs
- no token coins floating in space
- no aggressive motion lines implying hype

## 6. Copy-to-layout constraints

These limits keep brand tone aligned with the Light Premium Bento layout system.

### 6.1 Headline constraints

| Element | Japanese guidance | English guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Home hero headline | max 2 lines, ideally 14-22 full-width chars per line | max 2 lines, ideally 4-7 words per line |
| Standard page H1 | max 20 full-width chars | max 6 words |
| Section title | max 18 full-width chars | max 5 words |
| Card title | max 16 full-width chars | max 4 words |

### 6.2 Supporting copy constraints

| Element | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Hero subtitle | 2-3 sentences max, or 3 short lines max |
| Section subtitle | 1-2 sentences |
| Card description | 40-80 Japanese characters or 12-24 English words |
| CTA support text | 1 sentence |
| Tag/eyebrow | 1-3 short words or a very short phrase |

### 6.3 Copy structure preferences
Prefer:
- benefit first
- system/process second
- proof third

Avoid:
- feature dumping before the reader understands the business value
- long paragraphs in hero zones
- multiple competing CTAs in one card

## 7. Do / Don't guidance

### Pair 1 — AI messaging
**Do**  
Use language like:  
`AIを、現場で使える仕組みに落とし込む`  
`業務知識を、再利用できる運用資産に変える`

**Don't**  
Lead with vague future-talk like:  
`AIが世界を変える`  
`次世代の可能性を無限に拡張する`

**Why**  
Nebula Infinity wins on implementation credibility, not abstract futurism.

### Pair 2 — Workflow positioning
**Do**  
Show workflow transformation as:
`属人業務 → ルール化 → AI支援 → 組織資産化`

**Don't**  
Reduce workflow capability to only:
`AIで開発を高速化します`

**Why**  
Current development workflow examples are proof points, not the service boundary.

### Pair 3 — AI-Driven Development expression
**Do**  
Frame AI-driven development as:
`速さと品質を両立した、確かな実装`

**Don't**  
Frame it as:
`速さだけを誇張するハイプ`

**Why**  
V1 must signal reliable delivery, not hype.

### Pair 4 — Visual styling
**Do**  
Use light surfaces, navy structure, thin gold emphasis, modular cards, and soft gradients.

**Don't**  
Use neon glows, black glassmorphism, rainbow gradients, or high-contrast sci-fi effects.

**Why**  
The site should feel premium and trustworthy, not trend-chasing.

### Pair 5 — Proof communication
**Do**  
Use named proof like Japan Life Navi, Rigel, and Astra as evidence of broader capability.

**Don't**  
Make one project feel like the entire company.

**Why**  
Each case should prove capability without narrowing the brand.

## 8. Brand guardrails for V1

1. **Exactly three service lines must be visible across future design work.**
2. **No blog/news voice or editorial-magazine visual patterns** in V1.
3. **Bilingual parity matters.** Japanese and English versions should feel equally premium and equally clear.
4. **Case proof should feel selective and credible.** Fewer, stronger examples beat noisy galleries.
5. **Contact should feel business-ready.** The inquiry experience should sound clear, real, and low-friction.

---

## 9. Quick approval checklist

Use this before approving any future page design:

- Does it feel like a serious implementation partner?
- Is gold used for emphasis rather than decoration?
- Is the page visually structured enough to communicate intelligence?
- Does AI workflow read as organizational assetization, not only automation?
- Does AI-Driven Development read as fast, reliable delivery — not hype?
- Are icons consistent and emoji-free?
- Is the copy specific, restrained, and proof-aware?
- Is there any accidental blog/news direction creeping in?
